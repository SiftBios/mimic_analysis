What is domain enrichment?

This analysis compares the frequency of protein domains in mimic-bearing sequences with their frequency in the global metagenome.

  • Enrichment > 1: Domain is overrepresented in mimic-bearing proteins compared to the background metagenome
  • Enrichment < 1: Domain is underrepresented in mimic-bearing proteins

High enrichment values may indicate domains that are particularly important for MHC binding or interaction with the host immune system.

Enrichment Analysis Explanation

Domain enrichment analysis compares the frequency of protein domains in mimic-bearing sequences with their frequency in the global metagenome. This comparison helps identify which domains are overrepresented or underrepresented in sequences that contain MHC-I binding peptides.

Calculation Method

For each domain, we calculate:

Enrichment = (Domain count in mimic proteins / Total domains in mimic proteins) / (Domain count in metagenome / Total domains in metagenome)
Interpretation
  • Enrichment > 1: The domain is overrepresented in mimic-bearing proteins
  • Enrichment = 1: The domain occurs at the same frequency in both datasets
  • Enrichment < 1: The domain is underrepresented in mimic-bearing proteins
  • Enrichment = : The domain appears only in mimic-bearing proteins, not in the background metagenome
Significance

Domains with high enrichment values may play important roles in:

  • Generating peptides that mimic host epitopes
  • Interacting with the host immune system
  • Facilitating protein degradation patterns that expose MHC-binding peptides

Conversely, domains with low enrichment values (depletion) may be associated with proteins that are less likely to generate MHC-binding peptides, possibly due to:

  • Structural properties that protect against proteasomal degradation
  • Lack of sequences that can bind to MHC molecules
  • Functional roles that don't involve host-microbe interactions
